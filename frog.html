ALTIUS LAB CONCEPT DESIGN

Frog internship - Envisioning the digital environments for future biomedical lab.

Project Introduction

Altius is a cutting-edge research institute for biomedical science. John, the lab president and scientific director, also our primary stakeholder, wants us to help design a futuristic bio-medical lab. Before I joined this project, our team has met with John several times to align the vision - to design a place, toolset and culture that changes the paradigm of scientific research, collaboration and communication.

Design Process

In this project, I mainly participated in brainstorming, storytelling and wireframing. We do standup and iterate every day. We also meet with our stakeholders on a regular basis to gather feedback and inputs to our design iteration.

Brainstorm - research and collaboration

We first had two brainstorming sessions to generate ideas on improving research and collaboration. We posted our thoughts on each ideas and then selected and combined some concepts to continue storytelling. The concepts we landed on include: idea book, story board, knowledge network and lab book.

Storytelling

Storytelling is a big part of design in agency, we presented our ideas by storytelling to our clients. The concept I worked on is "Idea Book".

Wireframe

I transformed the "idea book" concept to wireframe. The wireframe includes idea capture, sharing and collaboration on both mobile and desktop.

Visual Deliverable

Our visual designer Sue designed the visual patterns for this project. And we all aligned our wireframes to her visual style. I also produced the interaction documentation.

Outcome and Reflection

The phase one deliverable for our client is a visually refined story that includes all the concepts we designed. However, as they don't have enough funding to build out all the concepts in phase two, we scoped things down to mostly establish a paradignm and guideline using existing technology and tools.

Interning at a design agency is very exciting because I have the chance to work on very interesting projects with least constraints. Yet the downside is that, as shown in this project, we can't guarantee the project will be built and shipped as we expected and we are not involved in the whole development cycle.
