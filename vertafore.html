Vertafore Experience Design

All About Iterative Problem Solving

Challenge I

How can we use cards to balance between tiles and panels in our platform?

Vertafore is integrating their major products into a platform so that users don't need to switch back and forth when they are multi-tasking. Currently we have tiles and panels as information container in our platform. Tiles only give a peak to small fraction of highlighted information about KPI. And panels contain too much information and complex interaction and the things users really care got buried. Also our panel is arbitrary size, and when users change one size, the others change too. So we propose card as an in-between information level, which can also be composed more gracefully.

With cards, our platform can be composed more neatly, and users can get the right amount of information and do quick interaction directly from the home page of platform and leave complicated job in subpages, so that they can get a holistic view first and prioritize their focus.

We explored the card anatomy and different canvas layouts as well as a cross-industry study.

Challenge II

What are the touch interaction standards as we move to mobile?

Most of our products are webapps. We are now moving to mobile, as more of our clients now works on tablets or phones in the field. The touch interaction framework we are using is Hammer.JS, thus we need to figure out what are the iOS and Android standards within the limitation of Hammer.JS, and map them to mouse click and keyboard shortcuts.

Challenge III

How is Client Portal performing and how do users interact with it?

Our XD team recently started using Adobe Analytics to study user behavior. The analysis can be used to validate and guide our design. Client Portal, which is a standalone product that allows users to manage their policies and communicate with their agents, is one of our first products that implemented Adobe Analytics tags, and I analyzed the data from March 21 release. The analysis is presented to our internal XD team as well as product and development team. I also built a standard scalable dashboard template for future analysis.
